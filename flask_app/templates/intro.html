Wind Turbine Energy Prediction Based on Weather Conditions

Renewable energy, such as wind and solar energy, plays an increasing role in the energy supply of the world. This trend will continue because global energy demand is increasing, and the use of nuclear power and traditional sources of energy like coal and oil is unsafe and leads to large COâ‚‚ emissions.

However, energy production from wind energy has grown rapidly in Europe, and the capacity of wind energy production has doubled from 2009 to 2010.

The levels of production of wind energy are hard to predict due to the potentially unstable weather conditions present at the wind farm. In particular, wind speed is crucial for energy production based on wind, and it may vary daily.

Energy suppliers are interested in accurate predictions to avoid overproduction by coordinating the collaborative production of traditional plants and weather-dependent energy sources. The energy can be predicted using the power curve and the wind speed.
